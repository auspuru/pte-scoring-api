🏗️The T.O.P.I.C. Framework
Every high-scoring SWT summary follows this 5-element structure. Cover all 5 in one sentence.
T
Topic
What is this about?
O
Opinion
What does the author believe?
P
Positives
What supports the argument?
I
Issues
What challenges exist?
C
Conclusion
What's the final point?
Attempt 10 — SWT 1 (Score: 86 Writing):
"The author made a lifestyle decision [T] to exchange a city terrace for a farm cottage, and he knew it was a good decision because he had been there in the past [O]; however, he was aware of the minor downsides of country living [I]; moreover, there were several benefits [P]; therefore, he tried to convince his wife that exchanging a warm large town house for a windswept farm cottage on a lower income was a good idea [C]."
⚖️Inclusion & Exclusion Criteria

✅ Always Include

✓Main topic / subject (first 1-2 sentences of passage)
✓Author's position or main argument
✓2–3 key supporting points (evidence/facts)
✓Conclusion / final outcome
✓Essential cause-effect relationships

❌ Always Exclude

✗Specific examples, anecdotes, stories
✗Repetitive or redundant information
✗Minor adjectives & background detail
✗Personal opinions or emotional language
✗Specific dates / names (unless essential)
Generalise, don't remove. "uncertain water supply, absence of central heating, single-track road blocked by snow…" → "minor disadvantages of country living"

📋Templates by Passage Type

Type 1 — Argumentative / Opinion

[The author/subject] argues/believes that [main argument], and [supporting point];
; However, [counterpoint/challenge];
; Moreover, [additional point];
; Therefore, [conclusion].

Type 2 — Descriptive / Informational

[The subject] is/are [description], and [characteristic 1];
; Moreover, [characteristic 2];
; Furthermore, [characteristic 3];
; Therefore, [significance/conclusion].

Type 3 — Process / Cause-Effect

[The process/event] results from [cause], and [effect 1];
; Consequently, [effect 2];
; Moreover, [additional effect];
; Therefore, [final outcome].

Type 4 — Historical / Development

[The subject] originated/began as [origin], and [early development];
; However, [challenge/setback];
; Moreover, [current status];
and despite [obstacle], [conclusion].
📏7 Do's & 8 Don'ts
✅ Do These
  • 1Write exactly one sentence — form score is 2 points; two sentences = zero
  • 2Stay between 5–75 words — target 40–65 for a safe buffer
  • 3Use 3–4 formal connectors joined by semicolons
  • 4Include 3–5 synonym swaps — not zero, not too many
  • 5Capitalise after semicolons — "; However," "; Moreover,"
  • 6Maintain original meaning — never change what the author says
  • 7Start with the main subject — "The author…" or "The [topic]…"
❌ Avoid These
  • 1Don't copy entire sentences verbatim from the passage
  • 2Don't use bullet points or numbered lists
  • 3Don't include your own opinion — use "The author argues…"
  • 4Don't change technical terms — generalise around them
  • 5Don't use informal connectors — but, also, so, besides
  • 6Don't write multiple sentences — use semicolons instead
  • 7Don't include every specific detail — generalise them
  • 8Don't exceed 75 words — instant zero for Form score

✅Pre-Submit Checklist — Run This Before Every Verify
Tick each item off before you hit Verify. This takes under 30 seconds and will catch most common mistakes.
Form (2 pts)
✓
My summary is exactly ONE sentence (no full stops except at the very end)
✓
Word count is between 5 and 75 words (target: 40–65)
✓
The summary ends with a period (.)
✓
The first word is capitalised
Connectors & Grammar (2 pts)
✓
I have used at least one formal connector (however, moreover, therefore, furthermore…)
✓
Every connector is preceded by a semicolon ("; However," not "However,")
✓
The word after the semicolon is capitalised ("; Moreover," ✓ "; moreover," ✗)
✓
No subject-verb agreement errors (they is → they are)
✓
No repeated words next to each other
Vocabulary (2 pts)
✓
I have swapped at least 3–5 words with meaning-safe synonyms
✓
None of my synonyms reverse or change the meaning (minor → small ✓, minor → major ✗)
✓
No first-person language ("I", "my", "we") unless it was in the passage AND I've shifted to third-person ("The author…")
Content (2 pts)
✓
My summary includes the TOPIC (main subject — what the passage is about)
✓
My summary includes the PIVOT / contrast (the "however" moment)
✓
My summary includes the CONCLUSION (the "so what" — final outcome or argument)
✓
I have NOT included specific dates, names, or minor examples
✓
I have NOT included my own opinion
Final Check
✓
I have read the summary aloud (in my head) — it makes sense as a single sentence
✓
The summary starts with the main subject ("The author…" / "The study…" / "The passage…")
✓
No spelling errors (I used the Spell Check button on the portal)
